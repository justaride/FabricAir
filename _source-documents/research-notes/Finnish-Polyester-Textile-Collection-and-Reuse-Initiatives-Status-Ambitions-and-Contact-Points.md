# Finnish Polyester Textile Collection and Reuse Initiatives: Status, Ambitions, and Contact Points

## Executive Summary

Finland operates Europe&#39;s most advanced textile recycling ecosystem, with mandatory separate collection implemented in January 2023—two years ahead of EU requirements\. The system processes 70\-100 million kilograms of textile waste annually through integrated public\-private partnerships, with specific capabilities for polyester and polyester\-blend recycling at industrial scale\.

## Major Public Initiatives

### 1\. Lounais\-Suomen Jätehuolto \(LSJH\) \- National Collection Coordinator

**Status:** Fully operational as Finland&#39;s primary coordinator of nationwide textile collection since 2023\. Currently building a €20\.5 million full\-scale textile refinement plant in Turku \(Topinpuisto\), expected completion 2025\-2026\. Processing approximately 6,300 tonnes annually with capacity expanding to handle 10% of Finland&#39;s textile waste\. [Source: Global\-recycling](https://global-recycling.info/archives/3218), [LSJH](https://poistotekstiili.lsjh.fi/en/home/)

**Ambition:** Create unified operations model covering 95% of Finland&#39;s population \(4\.5\-5\.4 million residents\) through 28 municipal waste management companies\. Establish Finland as world&#39;s first country with comprehensive textile collection for recycling\. Plans to serve broader Baltic region as processing hub\. [Source: Telaketju](https://telaketju.turkuamk.fi/en/the-telaketju-network/), [Scandinavian MIND](https://scandinavianmind.com/teemu-koskela-anna-garton-finland-headstart-in-recycling/)

**Polyester Capability:** Advanced sorting using NIR spectroscopy for fiber identification, specifically isolating polyester and polyester\-blend streams for downstream processing\. [Source: EastCham Finland](https://www.eastcham.fi/finnishwastemanagement/municipal-solid-waste/recycling-and-recovery/textile/)

## Major Private Initiatives

### 1\. Rester Oy \- Industrial\-Scale Mechanical Recycling Hub

**Status:** Fully operational since 2021, operating Northern Europe&#39;s largest mechanical textile recycling facility in Paimio\. Current capacity 6,000 tonnes annually, expanding to 12,000 tonnes\. In 2023, acquired LSJH&#39;s pilot fiber\-opening line, consolidating position as specialist in industrial\-scale mechanical processing\. [Source: Sitra](https://www.sitra.fi/en/cases/rester-is-finlands-first-large-scale-textile-recycling-plant-we-want-to-revolutionise-the-raw-materials-of-textiles-in-industry/), [Yarnsandfibers](https://www.yarnsandfibers.com/news/textile-news/rester-oy-finlands-pioneer-in-large-scale-textile-recycling)

**Ambition:** Become &quot;leading global forerunner in building textile recovery value chains\.&quot; Achieve 100% mechanical recycling without water or chemicals, saving 525,000 liters of water per processed tonne\. Process both closed\-loop \(company\-specific\) and open\-loop recycling models\. [Source: VisitPaimio](https://visitpaimio.fi/en/yritys/rester-oy-2/), [Rester](https://rester.fi/en/)

**Polyester Capability:** Explicitly processes polyester and cotton\-polyester blends from workwear\. Partnership with Vileda Professional for polyester&#x2F;polyester\-polypropylene production by\-streams\. Produces recycled fiber meeting Global Recycled Standard\. [Source: Rester](https://rester.fi/news/), [Touchpoint](https://www.touchpointww.com/news/closing-the-loop-touchpoint-launches-new-workwear-fabric-retouch)

## Regulatory Framework

Finland implemented mandatory separate textile collection January 1, 2023—two years ahead of EU&#39;s 2025 requirement\. Extended Producer Responsibility \(EPR\) for textiles expected 2027\-2028, managed by Pirkanmaan ELY\-keskus\. [Source: EastCham Finland](https://www.eastcham.fi/), [European Commission](https://ec.europa.eu/)\]
