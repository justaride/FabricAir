# Q4: Regulations — Norway

> What regulations govern polyester textile disposal/reuse in Norway, and how will they evolve?

---

## Key Finding

**Norway has mandatory textile collection from January 2025, but EPR implementation date is uncertain.** Originally expected 2027, it may be delayed to 2028 pending policy review.

---

## Current Regulatory Framework

| Regulation | Status | Impact |
|------------|--------|--------|
| Organic waste landfill ban | In force (2009) | Textiles to incineration/export |
| Waste hierarchy | In force | Prevention > Reuse > Recycling |
| Producer responsibility (general) | In force | Extends to textiles (date TBD) |

## Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR)

### What EPR Will Mean

| Aspect | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| **Who pays** | Producers/importers of textiles |
| **For what** | End-of-life collection and treatment |
| **Fee structure** | Based on volume and recyclability |
| **Targets** | Recycling percentages (TBD) |

## Strategic Implications

1. **EPR uncertainty is a risk** — timing affects business planning
2. **Collection increasing** — feedstock availability will grow
3. **First-mover advantage** — establish recycling before EPR creates competition
4. **GRS certification** — essential for commercial credibility
