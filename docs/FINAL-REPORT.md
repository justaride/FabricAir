## Part 1: Finland Analysis

*Full 6-question analysis: [Finland Q1-Q6](02-research-questions/index.md)*

### 1.1 Market Overview

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Textile waste | 85,770 t/year | Turku UAS |
| Textile services market | €350-450M | Industry data |
| Material recycling | <1% | SYKE |
| EPR date | 2027-28 | Government |
| Mandatory collection | Since Jan 2023 | Finnish law |

Finland leads Europe with mandatory textile collection 2 years ahead of EU requirements.

### 1.2 Best Sources for 100% White Polyester

| Priority | Sector | White % | 100% PET | Key Players |
|----------|--------|---------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Pharma/Cleanroom | >90% | Yes | Orion, Okmetic, Murata |
| 2 | Food Processing | 70-80% | Mixed | Valio, Fazer, Atria |
| 3 | Healthcare | ~60% | Often blends | HUS, Terveystalo |
| 4 | Industrial | <10% | Mixed | YIT, KONE, Wärtsilä |

**Key Insight:** Most Finnish workwear is 65/35 poly-cotton—NOT suitable for FabricAir. Target cleanroom and pharmaceutical sectors.

### 1.3 Value Chain Stakeholders

```
Production → Use → Collection → Sorting → Recycling → New Products
```

**Key Players by Role:**

| Role | Organization | Market Position |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| Recycling | **Rester** | Only large-scale recycler |
| Textile Services | Puro, SOL, Elis | Control ~70% of workwear |
| Manufacturing | Image Wear, Touchpoint | Workwear production |
| Research | VTT, Aalto, Telaketju | Technology development |

**Critical:** ~~Lindström~~ uses 65:35 poly-cotton blends—NOT compatible with FabricAir requirements.

### 1.4 Disposal Routes

| Route | Percentage | Volume |
|-------|------------|--------|
| Energy Recovery | 60% | ~51,000 t |
| Material Recovery | 22% | ~19,000 t |
| Export | 18% | ~15,300 t |
| **Fiber-to-Fiber** | **<1%** | — |

Professional workwear streams (1,330 t/year from hotels/restaurants/hospitals) offer controlled quality but most is currently incinerated.

### 1.5 Regulatory Timeline

| Date | Regulation | Status |
|------|------------|--------|

| 2026-27 | Digital Product Passport | Upcoming |
| **2027-28** | **EPR full implementation** | **Upcoming** |

EPR transition will transform the market—companies will shift from paying for disposal to selling textile waste.

## Part 5: Risk Assessment

| Risk | Level | Mitigation |
|------|-------|------------|
| Rester capacity locked by Lindström | High | Engage immediately; explore NTG alternative |
| 65/35 blend contamination | High | Require certified 100% PET; target cleanroom |
| Quality variability | Medium | Rester pre-sorting; specify white only |
| rPET price premium (50-70%) | Medium | Volume commitments for better pricing |
| Rewin delay | Medium | Maintain Rester relationship as backup |
| Norway EPR uncertainty | Medium | Monitor government announcements |

### Key Risks by Country

**Finland:**
- Lindström has locked Rester capacity via shareholding
- Limited recycling alternatives if Rester unavailable

**Sweden:**
- Rewin not operational until October 2027
- Renewcell bankruptcy shows market volatility

**Norway:**
- EPR timeline uncertain (under government review)
- NTG scaling depends on funding
