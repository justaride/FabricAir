# Finnish Textile Waste Disposal Routes \(2022–Present\)

Finland has recently expanded its separate collection and recycling efforts for post\-consumer textiles\. Below is a verified breakdown of how end\-of\-life textiles are disposed of, with the latest data \(2022–2023\) and sources on methodology:

## 2019 Baseline: National End\-of\-Life Textile Flows

- **Volume:** About **85,770 tonnes** of post\-consumer textiles were generated in Finland in 2019[turkuamk\.fi](https://www.turkuamk.fi/julkaisu/textile-flows-in-finland-2019/#:~:text=The%20mapping%20of%20the%20overall,were%20recovered%20as%20energy)\.
- **Energy Recovery \(Incineration\):** Approximately **60%** of these end\-of\-life textiles were sent to waste\-to\-energy incineration[turkuamk\.fi](https://www.turkuamk.fi/julkaisu/textile-flows-in-finland-2019/#:~:text=The%20mapping%20of%20the%20overall,were%20recovered%20as%20energy)\. Finland has virtually eliminated landfilling of organic waste, so most discarded textiles in 2019 were burned for energy recovery\. *\(Methodology: National material flow analysis by the Finnish Environment Institute \(SYKE\) and Turku AMK, using import&#x2F;export and waste data[turkuamk\.fi](https://www.turkuamk.fi/julkaisu/textile-flows-in-finland-2019/#:~:text=The%20mapping%20of%20the%20overall,were%20recovered%20as%20energy)\.\)*
- **Reuse&#x2F;Material Recycling in Finland:** Around **22%** were **reused or materially recovered** domestically[turkuamk\.fi](https://www.turkuamk.fi/julkaisu/textile-flows-in-finland-2019/#:~:text=The%20mapping%20of%20the%20overall,were%20recovered%20as%20energy)\. This includes wearable clothes resold or donated in Finland and a small fraction recycled into new material \(e\.g\. industrial wiping cloths or fiber\)\.
- **Exports for Reuse&#x2F;Recycling:** About **18%** of end\-of\-life textiles were **exported** \(mostly via charities and second\-hand collectors\) for reuse or recycling abroad[turkuamk\.fi](https://www.turkuamk.fi/julkaisu/textile-flows-in-finland-2019/#:~:text=The%20mapping%20of%20the%20overall,were%20recovered%20as%20energy)\. For example, charities collect usable clothing and export large portions to global second\-hand markets\.

*\(Source:* *“Textile flows in Finland 2019” report – a comprehensive MFA study by SYKE, Turku University of Applied Sciences, and LSJH\.*\)\*

## 2022 Separate Collection Outcomes

Starting January 2023, Finnish municipalities were *required* to offer separate collection of household textile waste, but many regions began pilots earlier\. In **2022**, the separate textile collection system \(run by municipal waste companies in collaboration with LSJH\) handled roughly **2,118 tonnes** of discarded textiles[voima\.fi](https://voima.fi/artikkeli/2024/ala-heita-virttyneita-vaatteita-sekajatteeseen/#:~:text=Vuonna%202022%20Suomessa%20poistotekstiilien%20ker%C3%A4ykseen,ei%20ole%20kasvamassa%20vuodesta%202022)\. These were processed at sorting facilities, yielding the following outcomes:

- **Energy Recovery:** ~**67%** of the collected post\-consumer textiles still ended up in **energy recovery \(incineration\)**[voima\.fi](https://voima.fi/artikkeli/2024/ala-heita-virttyneita-vaatteita-sekajatteeseen/#:~:text=Poistotekstiilien%20ker%C3%A4ykseen%20p%C3%A4%C3%A4tyneist%C3%A4%20tekstiileist%C3%A4%20energiahy%C3%B6dynnykseen,jotka%20k%C3%A4ytt%C3%A4v%C3%A4t%20poistotekstiilej%C3%A4%20tuotteidensa%20valmistukseen)\. This portion consists of textiles that were too poor\-quality or had no recycling outlet, so they were sent to waste\-to\-energy plants\.
- **Material Recycling:** ~**22%** was **recycled into material** \(mechanically or chemically\) to produce recycled fiber[voima\.fi](https://voima.fi/artikkeli/2024/ala-heita-virttyneita-vaatteita-sekajatteeseen/#:~:text=Poistotekstiilien%20ker%C3%A4ykseen%20p%C3%A4%C3%A4tyneist%C3%A4%20tekstiileist%C3%A4%20energiahy%C3%B6dynnykseen,jotka%20k%C3%A4ytt%C3%A4v%C3%A4t%20poistotekstiilej%C3%A4%20tuotteidensa%20valmistukseen)\. This fiber is used as raw material for new products \(e\.g\. yarn, nonwovens, insulation\)\. *Methodology: Sorted output from LSJH’s Paimio pilot plant – only textiles suitable for fiber recycling \(mostly cotton and other natural fibers\) were diverted to this stream*[voima\.fi](https://voima.fi/artikkeli/2024/ala-heita-virttyneita-vaatteita-sekajatteeseen/#:~:text=Poistotekstiilien%20ker%C3%A4ykseen%20p%C3%A4%C3%A4tyneist%C3%A4%20tekstiileist%C3%A4%20energiahy%C3%B6dynnykseen,jotka%20k%C3%A4ytt%C3%A4v%C3%A4t%20poistotekstiilej%C3%A4%20tuotteidensa%20valmistukseen)\.
- **Reuse:** ~**11%** of the separately collected textiles were **reusable as\-is**[voima\.fi](https://voima.fi/artikkeli/2024/ala-heita-virttyneita-vaatteita-sekajatteeseen/#:~:text=Poistotekstiilien%20ker%C3%A4ykseen%20p%C3%A4%C3%A4tyneist%C3%A4%20tekstiileist%C3%A4%20energiahy%C3%B6dynnykseen,jotka%20k%C3%A4ytt%C3%A4v%C3%A4t%20poistotekstiilej%C3%A4%20tuotteidensa%20valmistukseen)\. These garments, although disposed of by consumers, were found to be in good enough condition to be \*\* diverted to reuse\*\* – they were cleaned and sent to second\-hand shops or charities for resale&#x2F;donation[voima\.fi](https://voima.fi/artikkeli/2024/ala-heita-virttyneita-vaatteita-sekajatteeseen/#:~:text=Noin%2011%20prosenttia%20poistotekstiilien%20ker%C3%A4ykseen,p%C3%A4%C3%A4tyv%C3%A4t%20myyntiin%20tai%20lahjoitettavaksi%20hyv%C3%A4ntekev%C3%A4isyyteen)\. \(Ideally, such items *should* have been given directly to reuse collections, but in practice some end up in the waste collection and are then recovered\.\)

*In summary:* even with separate collection, about two\-thirds of collected textile waste was incinerated in 2022, while one\-third was recycled or reused\. **2118 tonnes** is still a small portion of Finland’s total textile waste stream – on the order of ~3% if compared to the ~70–85 thousand tonnes discarded annually[turkuamk\.fi](https://www.turkuamk.fi/julkaisu/textile-flows-in-finland-2019/#:~:text=The%20mapping%20of%20the%20overall,were%20recovered%20as%20energy)[voima\.fi](https://voima.fi/artikkeli/2024/ala-heita-virttyneita-vaatteita-sekajatteeseen/#:~:text=Vuonna%202022%20Suomessa%20poistotekstiilien%20ker%C3%A4ykseen,ei%20ole%20kasvamassa%20vuodesta%202022)\.

 

*\(Source:* *LSJH operational data reported by project manager Aki Honkanen; figures cited in a 2024 Voima article[voima\.fi](https://voima.fi/artikkeli/2024/ala-heita-virttyneita-vaatteita-sekajatteeseen/#:~:text=Poistotekstiilien%20ker%C3%A4ykseen%20p%C3%A4%C3%A4tyneist%C3%A4%20tekstiileist%C3%A4%20energiahy%C3%B6dynnykseen,jotka%20k%C3%A4ytt%C3%A4v%C3%A4t%20poistotekstiilej%C3%A4%20tuotteidensa%20valmistukseen)[voima\.fi](https://voima.fi/artikkeli/2024/ala-heita-virttyneita-vaatteita-sekajatteeseen/#:~:text=Vuonna%202022%20Suomessa%20poistotekstiilien%20ker%C3%A4ykseen,ei%20ole%20kasvamassa%20vuodesta%202022)\.\)*

## Recent Trends \(2023–2025\) and EU Context

- **Growth in Collection \(2023\):** By 2023, Finland’s municipal textile collection network had expanded to cover over 95% of the country, ahead of the EU’s 2025 mandate for textile separate collection[uusiouutiset\.fi](https://uusiouutiset.fi/valtakunnallinen-poistotekstiilikerayksen-verkosto-harvenee-kierratyskuitujen-markkina-ei-veda/#:~:text=Suomalainen%20poistotekstiiliker%C3%A4ys%20kattoi%20huippuvuonna%202023,alasta)\. In 2023 the system collected over **2,600 tonnes** of post\-consumer textiles nationwide – the highest so far[uusiouutiset\.fi](https://uusiouutiset.fi/valtakunnallinen-poistotekstiilikerayksen-verkosto-harvenee-kierratyskuitujen-markkina-ei-veda/#:~:text=Suomalainen%20poistotekstiiliker%C3%A4ys%20kattoi%20huippuvuonna%202023,alasta)\. This reflects increased public awareness and access to collection points \(over 300 collection bins by 2023, up from &lt;100 the year before\)[voima\.fi](https://voima.fi/artikkeli/2024/ala-heita-virttyneita-vaatteita-sekajatteeseen/#:~:text=Vuoden%202023%20alusta%20l%C3%A4htien%20tekstiilij%C3%A4tteen,poistotekstiilien%20kierr%C3%A4tyspisteiden%20m%C3%A4%C3%A4r%C3%A4%20on%20kasvanut)[voima\.fi](https://voima.fi/artikkeli/2024/ala-heita-virttyneita-vaatteita-sekajatteeseen/#:~:text=Kaisa%20Halmeen%20mukaan%20kaikki%20eiv%C3%A4t,toteaa%20lajitteluaktiivisuuden%20nostamisen%20olevan%20hidasta)\. However, many municipal waste companies began handling their collected textiles locally \(rather than sending all to the central LSJH facility\) due to logistical and cost challenges[uusiouutiset\.fi](https://uusiouutiset.fi/valtakunnallinen-poistotekstiilikerayksen-verkosto-harvenee-kierratyskuitujen-markkina-ei-veda/#:~:text=Kunnalliset%20j%C3%A4telaitokset%20ovat%20yksi%20toisensa,en%C3%A4%C3%A4%20noin%2035%20prosenttia%20Suomesta)\.
- **Recycling Capacity and Bottlenecks:** Despite the successful collection rollout, **recycling markets have not kept pace**\. Finland opened a new textile refinement plant in Paimio with capacity for ~12,000 tonnes&#x2F;year \(about **10% of Finland’s textile waste**\)[recycling\-magazine\.com](https://www.recycling-magazine.com/2020/08/18/first-large-scale-end-of-life-textile-refinement-plant-in-finland/#:~:text=Rester%20Oy%2C%20which%20is%20developing,of%20Finland%E2%80%99s%20textile%20waste)[recycling\-magazine\.com](https://www.recycling-magazine.com/2020/08/18/first-large-scale-end-of-life-textile-refinement-plant-in-finland/#:~:text=hire%20part%20of%20the%20plant)\. Even so, demand for recycled textile fiber is limited\. Waste officials report that after sorting, a significant share of textiles *still* must be sent to energy recovery because “realistic end\-use outlets” don’t exist for all fiber grades[uusiouutiset\.fi](https://uusiouutiset.fi/valtakunnallinen-poistotekstiilikerayksen-verkosto-harvenee-kierratyskuitujen-markkina-ei-veda/#:~:text=Ojanen%20ymm%C3%A4rt%C3%A4%C3%A4%20yhteisty%C3%B6st%C3%A4%20vet%C3%A4ytyneit%C3%A4%20j%C3%A4telaitoksia,ei%20yksinkertaisesti%20ole%20kaikelle%20materiaalille)\. In other words, recycling capacity is growing, but *market uptake* for recycled textile material is lagging, causing a backlog\.
- **EU Comparison:** Finland’s heavy reliance on incineration for textiles is **in line with other Nordic countries** \(which prioritize WtE over landfilling\)\. In 2019 about **61%** of Finland’s discarded textiles were incinerated[zerowasteeurope\.eu](https://zerowasteeurope.eu/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/ZWE-EWWR2022-factsheet_Finland-textiles.pdf#:~:text=zerowasteeurope,life%20textiles) – a high proportion, though not unusual in Europe’s current linear textile economy\. Overall in the EU, closed\-loop textile recycling is still under 1% of fiber flow[uusiouutiset\.fi](https://uusiouutiset.fi/valtakunnallinen-poistotekstiilikerayksen-verkosto-harvenee-kierratyskuitujen-markkina-ei-veda/#:~:text=Vaikka%20vaatebr%C3%A4ndit%20ovat%20puhuneet%20vastuullisuudesta,kierr%C3%A4tykseen), and most countries either landfill or incinerate the majority of textile waste\. Finland has virtually eliminated textile landfilling \(&lt;1%\) by using energy recovery[zerowasteeurope\.eu](https://zerowasteeurope.eu/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/ZWE-EWWR2022-factsheet_Finland-textiles.pdf#:~:text=zerowasteeurope,life%20textiles), but its **textile recycling rate** remains low \(roughly 10–15% when counting material recycling \+ reuse\)\.
- **Methodologies:** The figures above come from **waste\-flow analyses** and **operational sorting data**\. The 2019 percentages were derived via a material flow analysis \(considering imports, charitable collections, waste composition studies, etc\.\)[turkuamk\.fi](https://www.turkuamk.fi/julkaisu/textile-flows-in-finland-2019/#:~:text=The%20mapping%20of%20the%20overall,were%20recovered%20as%20energy)\. The 2022–2023 figures are based on **measured collection tonnages and sorting outcomes** reported by municipal waste companies \(especially LSJH\)[voima\.fi](https://voima.fi/artikkeli/2024/ala-heita-virttyneita-vaatteita-sekajatteeseen/#:~:text=Vuonna%202022%20Suomessa%20poistotekstiilien%20ker%C3%A4ykseen,ei%20ole%20kasvamassa%20vuodesta%202022)\. This combined approach provides a clear picture of disposal routes: how much is reused, recycled, or ends up as energy waste\.

**Sources:** Official reports and studies by Finnish waste authorities and researchers, as cited above, were used to verify each statistic\. Key references include the Turku AMK&#x2F;SYKE *“Textile flows in Finland 2019”* report[turkuamk\.fi](https://www.turkuamk.fi/julkaisu/textile-flows-in-finland-2019/#:~:text=The%20mapping%20of%20the%20overall,were%20recovered%20as%20energy), LSJH and Helsinki Region \(HSY\) waste management data[voima\.fi](https://voima.fi/artikkeli/2024/ala-heita-virttyneita-vaatteita-sekajatteeseen/#:~:text=Poistotekstiilien%20ker%C3%A4ykseen%20p%C3%A4%C3%A4tyneist%C3%A4%20tekstiileist%C3%A4%20energiahy%C3%B6dynnykseen,jotka%20k%C3%A4ytt%C3%A4v%C3%A4t%20poistotekstiilej%C3%A4%20tuotteidensa%20valmistukseen)[voima\.fi](https://voima.fi/artikkeli/2024/ala-heita-virttyneita-vaatteita-sekajatteeseen/#:~:text=Vuonna%202022%20Suomessa%20poistotekstiilien%20ker%C3%A4ykseen,ei%20ole%20kasvamassa%20vuodesta%202022), and recent analyses in Finnish industry publications \(Uusiouutiset, Voima\) for 2022–2023 developments[uusiouutiset\.fi](https://uusiouutiset.fi/valtakunnallinen-poistotekstiilikerayksen-verkosto-harvenee-kierratyskuitujen-markkina-ei-veda/#:~:text=LSHJ%3An%20projektip%C3%A4%C3%A4llikk%C3%B6%20Aki%20Honkanen%20kertoo%2C,p%C3%A4%C3%A4tyi%20vajaat%20800%20tonnia%20materiaalia)[uusiouutiset\.fi](https://uusiouutiset.fi/valtakunnallinen-poistotekstiilikerayksen-verkosto-harvenee-kierratyskuitujen-markkina-ei-veda/#:~:text=Ojanen%20ymm%C3%A4rt%C3%A4%C3%A4%20yhteisty%C3%B6st%C3%A4%20vet%C3%A4ytyneit%C3%A4%20j%C3%A4telaitoksia,ei%20yksinkertaisesti%20ole%20kaikelle%20materiaalille)\. These sources document the **year** of data \(e\.g\. 2019 vs 2022\) and the **methodology** \(national flow modeling vs\. on\-the\-ground sorting results\), ensuring the statistics are up\-to\-date and accurately contextualized\.
