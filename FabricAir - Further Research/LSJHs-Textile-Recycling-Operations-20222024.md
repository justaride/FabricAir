# LSJH’s Textile Recycling Operations \(2022–2024\)

*Bales of post\-consumer textiles sorted at an LSJH facility\. Lounais\-Suomen Jätehuolto \(LSJH\) has led Finland’s pioneering effort to collect and recycle household textile waste nationwide\.*

## Planned Full\-Scale Textile Sorting &amp; Refinement Plant \(Topinpuisto, Turku\)

LSJH, the municipal waste management company for 18 Southwest Finnish municipalities, has been planning a **full\-scale end\-of\-life textile refinement plant** in the Topinpuisto circular economy hub in Turku\. Announced in early 2022, this facility was envisioned to make Finland the first country to collect and recycle post\-consumer textiles nationwide[topinpuisto\.fi](https://topinpuisto.fi/uutinen/topinpuistoon-tulee-poistotekstiilien-jalostuslaitos/#:~:text=Business%20Finland%20on%20my%C3%B6nt%C3%A4nyt%20Lounais,saadaan%20maanlaajuisesti%20uudelleenk%C3%A4ytt%C3%B6%C3%B6n%20ja%20kierr%C3%A4tykseen)[topinpuisto\.fi](https://topinpuisto.fi/uutinen/topinpuistoon-tulee-poistotekstiilien-jalostuslaitos/#:~:text=%E2%80%93%20Poistotekstiilin%20jalostuslaitoksen%20rakentaminen%20mahdollistaa,LSJH%3An%20toimitusjohtaja%20Jukka%20Heikkil%C3%A4%20iloitsee)\. Key specifications of the planned plant included:

- **Investment** – A total budget of approximately **€20\.5 million**, supported by a **€5\.2 million Business Finland circular economy grant**[topinpuisto\.fi](https://topinpuisto.fi/uutinen/topinpuistoon-tulee-poistotekstiilien-jalostuslaitos/#:~:text=Lounais,osuus%20noin%205%2C2%20miljoonaa%20euroa)[pciaw\.org](https://pciaw.org/end-of-life-textile-refinement-plant-receives-funding-from-business-finland/#:~:text=Lounais,2%20million%20euros)\. \(The grant was secured in 2022 to kick\-start the project\.\)
- **Processing Capacity** – Designed to handle about **20,000 tons of end\-of\-life textiles per year**, sufficient to process *all* post\-consumer textiles collected across Finland \(with potential to accept textile waste from the Baltic region as well\)[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/eun-tuottajavastuuvalmistelu-vesitti-poistotekstiilin-jalostuslaitoksen-rakentamisen-turkuun/#:~:text=Lounais,ole%20toiminnallisia%20tai%20taloudellisia%20edellytyksi%C3%A4)[global\-recycling\.info](https://global-recycling.info/archives/4180#:~:text=a%20larger%20industrial%20scale%2C%20the,life%20textile%20flows)\. This scale equates to the nationwide output of textile waste, positioning the plant as a central hub for textile recycling\.
- **Technology** – Planned to use **partly automated sorting lines with optical identification**\. LSJH has developed **near\-infrared \(NIR\) sensor technology** \(in partnership with Lahti UAS and Spectral Engines\) to sort textiles by fiber composition[global\-recycling\.info](https://global-recycling.info/archives/4180#:~:text=generated%20in%20Finland,the%20equipment%20supplier%2C%20Spectral%20Engines)\. In the Turku plant, incoming textiles would be **sorted by material type** via NIR\-based automation \(supplemented by some manual handling\), separating streams like cotton, polyester, etc\. for optimal reuse\. Sorted garments suitable for **reuse** would be diverted for resale, while the rest would undergo **mechanical recycling** – i\.e\. **fiber opening** \(shredding and pulling fibers\) to produce recycled textile fiber[topinpuisto\.fi](https://topinpuisto.fi/uutinen/topinpuistoon-tulee-poistotekstiilien-jalostuslaitos/#:~:text=Poistotekstiilin%20jalostuslaitoksessa%20kuluttajapoistotekstiili%20lajitellaan%20osin,akustiikkalevyjen%2C%20suodatinkankaiden%20ja%20komposiittien%20valmistukseen)[global\-recycling\.info](https://global-recycling.info/archives/4180#:~:text=has%20been%20developing%20the%20near,the%20equipment%20supplier%2C%20Spectral%20Engines)\. The recycled fiber could serve as raw material for new products such as yarns, nonwoven fabrics, insulation, filling materials, acoustic panels, filter cloths, and composites[topinpuisto\.fi](https://topinpuisto.fi/uutinen/topinpuistoon-tulee-poistotekstiilien-jalostuslaitos/#:~:text=Poistotekstiilin%20jalostuslaitoksessa%20kuluttajapoistotekstiili%20lajitellaan%20osin,akustiikkalevyjen%2C%20suodatinkankaiden%20ja%20komposiittien%20valmistukseen)\. This high\-tech sorting and fiber\-refining process was a centerpiece of the project’s innovation, ensuring high\-quality material recovery\.

**Timeline and Ecosystem Impact:** The construction was slated to begin by 2023–2024, with the **plant operational by 2025**[topinpuisto\.fi](https://topinpuisto.fi/uutinen/topinpuistoon-tulee-poistotekstiilien-jalostuslaitos/#:~:text=%E2%80%93%20Seuraavaksi%20laadimme%20ymp%C3%A4rist%C3%B6lupahakemuksen%20ja,vuoden%202025%20aikana%2C%20Heikkil%C3%A4%20pohtii)\. Once running at full capacity, the facility was expected to **employ about 100 people** directly by 2025[topinpuisto\.fi](https://topinpuisto.fi/uutinen/topinpuistoon-tulee-poistotekstiilien-jalostuslaitos/#:~:text=%E2%80%93%20Seuraavaksi%20laadimme%20ymp%C3%A4rist%C3%B6lupahakemuksen%20ja,vuoden%202025%20aikana%2C%20Heikkil%C3%A4%20pohtii)\. Moreover, it was projected to spur a broader circular textile economy in Finland – estimates by VTT suggested up to **17,000 new jobs** could be created across the value chain \(in recycling, manufacturing of new products from recycled fiber, etc\.\) thanks to this ecosystem[topinpuisto\.fi](https://topinpuisto.fi/uutinen/topinpuistoon-tulee-poistotekstiilien-jalostuslaitos/#:~:text=Kotimainen%20tekstiiliekosysteemi%20synnytt%C3%A4isi%20jopa%2017%C2%A0000,uutta%20ty%C3%B6paikkaa)[topinpuisto\.fi](https://topinpuisto.fi/uutinen/topinpuistoon-tulee-poistotekstiilien-jalostuslaitos/#:~:text=Jalostuslaitoksen%20arvioidaan%20vuonna%202025%20ty%C3%B6llist%C3%A4v%C3%A4n,jopa%2017%20000%20uutta%20ty%C3%B6paikkaa)\. The plant aimed to serve all Finnish municipal waste companies: LSJH would coordinate national collection logistics so that any municipality could send collected textiles to Turku for processing, and equally any company could purchase the resulting recycled fiber for their products[news\.spinverse\.com](https://news.spinverse.com/spinverse-helps-secure-5.2-m-of-funding-for-an-end-of-life-textile-refinement-plant-project#:~:text=According%20to%20LSJH%20CEO%20Jukka,production%20of%20their%20own%20products)[news\.spinverse\.com](https://news.spinverse.com/spinverse-helps-secure-5.2-m-of-funding-for-an-end-of-life-textile-refinement-plant-project#:~:text=LSJH%20has%20been%20establishing%20utilisation,of%20the%20new%20refinement%20plant)\. In summary, the Topinpuisto textile plant was envisioned as a national solution for textile waste, integrating cutting\-edge sorting technology with a circular business model\.

## Pilot Operations in Paimio \(Industrial\-Scale Testing\)

Before committing to the full\-scale facility, LSJH established an **industrial pilot plant** to develop and validate the textile recycling process\. This pilot, often referred to as the “Green Field Hub” in Paimio, began operations in **late 2021**[news\.spinverse\.com](https://news.spinverse.com/spinverse-helps-secure-5.2-m-of-funding-for-an-end-of-life-textile-refinement-plant-project#:~:text=LSJH%20has%20been%20establishing%20utilisation,of%20the%20new%20refinement%20plant)\. The Paimio pilot line focused on **mechanical fiber opening** of sorted textiles and allowed LSJH to fine\-tune collection and sorting methods\. Key points about the pilot phase:

- **Capacity &amp; Process:** The pilot facility, once fully ramped up, was capable of processing on the order of **5 million kg \(5,000 tons\) of textiles annually**[global\-recycling\.info](https://global-recycling.info/archives/4180#:~:text=At%20the%20plant%2C%20the%20end,from%20manual%20sorting%20towards%20an)\. It initially relied on **manual sorting** of incoming textiles, but one goal was to transition toward **automated NIR identification** on the pilot line[global\-recycling\.info](https://global-recycling.info/archives/4180#:~:text=future%20goal%20is%20to%20set,life%20textile%20flows)\. By piloting an automated sorting system, LSJH aimed to handle larger volumes and achieve the fiber purity needed for high\-quality recycling\. After sorting \(manual or automated\), textiles were mechanically shredded and opened into fiber, as a proof\-of\-concept that the fibers could meet industry requirements[global\-recycling\.info](https://global-recycling.info/archives/4180#:~:text=At%20the%20plant%2C%20the%20end,from%20manual%20sorting%20towards%20an)\.
- **Collection Network:** During 2022–2023, the Paimio pilot processed textiles collected from **10 different municipal waste companies’ regions across Finland**[news\.spinverse\.com](https://news.spinverse.com/spinverse-helps-secure-5.2-m-of-funding-for-an-end-of-life-textile-refinement-plant-project#:~:text=LSJH%20has%20been%20establishing%20utilisation,of%20the%20new%20refinement%20plant)\. This demonstrated the feasibility of a nationwide collection model feeding a centralized recycling plant\. LSJH coordinated with other regional waste firms \(through the Telaketju network and other collaborations\) to gather post\-consumer clothing and household textiles for the pilot\. By 2023, LSJH had also set up **separate textile collection points in every municipality of its operating area** \(as required by new waste regulations\)[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/LSJH-Vuosikatsaus-2023_nettiin.pdf#:~:text=siirtyi%20LSJH%3Alle%2C%20sek%C3%A4%20poistotekstiiliss%C3%A4%2C%20jolle,vaadittu%20muutosvaiheessa%20toimenpiteit%C3%A4%20ja%20paikoin)[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/LSJH-Vuosikatsaus-2023_nettiin.pdf#:~:text=toimialueemme%20kunnissa,vuosittain%20syntyv%C3%A4st%C3%A4%20yli%2012%20miljoonan)\.
- **Outcomes:** The pilot plant provided valuable data on sorting efficiency, fiber quality, and operational challenges\. LSJH gained “a lot of information” to guide the design of the full\-scale Turku plant, including insights into processing parameters and equipment needs[pciaw\.org](https://pciaw.org/end-of-life-textile-refinement-plant-receives-funding-from-business-finland/#:~:text=Mechanical%20fibre%20opening%20of%20end,textiles%20is%20piloted%20in%20Paimio)\. The **recycled fibers produced in Paimio** were supplied to various companies for product development – for example, Finnish textile innovators tested the material in applications like new yarns, fabrics, and insulation\. This phase also helped engage stakeholders: numerous textile and fiber technology companies partnered via **Telaketju** and **Telavalue** projects to explore uses for the material and to develop advanced sorting tech in tandem with LSJH[global\-recycling\.info](https://global-recycling.info/archives/4180#:~:text=The%20refinement%20plant%20project%20involves,investment%20aid%20by%20Business%20Finland)[telaketju\.turkuamk\.fi](https://telaketju.turkuamk.fi/en/the-telaketju-network/#:~:text=Lounais,life%20textiles)\.

It’s worth noting that LSJH also operates a **manual sorting center in Turku** \(separate from Paimio\) where textiles are pre\-sorted and baled[textended\.eu](https://textended.eu/lounais-suomen-jatehuolto/#:~:text=LSJH%E2%80%99s%20role%20in%20tExtended%20concentrates,textiles%20to%20create%20new%20markets)\. This Turku sorting facility handles the incoming stream of collected textiles, removing obviously reusable items and non\-textile impurities, before sending the rest for refinement\. Both the Turku sorting hub and the Paimio pilot line have functioned as **demonstration platforms** for new technologies \(e\.g\. testing sensor\-based sorting in real operating conditions\)[textended\.eu](https://textended.eu/lounais-suomen-jatehuolto/#:~:text=LSJH%E2%80%99s%20role%20in%20tExtended%20concentrates,textiles%20to%20create%20new%20markets)\.

## Recent Developments: Producer Responsibility and Project Status

In the past three years, the regulatory and market landscape for textile waste has evolved significantly, affecting LSJH’s project\. **EU and Finnish waste legislation introduced Extended Producer Responsibility \(EPR\) for textiles**, and from 2023 Finland mandated separate collection of post\-consumer textiles\. These changes have introduced uncertainty about who will finance and operate large\-scale sorting facilities\. Key recent developments include:

- **EU Textile EPR Rollout:** A new EU Waste Framework Directive amendment is being finalized to impose **harmonized producer responsibility for textiles** across member states[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/eun-tuottajavastuuvalmistelu-vesitti-poistotekstiilin-jalostuslaitoksen-rakentamisen-turkuun/#:~:text=%E2%80%93%20Lopullista%20p%C3%A4%C3%A4t%C3%B6ksentekoa%20laitosinvestoinnista%20on,kertoo%20LSJH%3An%20toimitusjohtaja%20Jukka%20Heikkil%C3%A4)[yle\.fi](https://yle.fi/a/74-20085331#:~:text=toistuvasti%20siirtyneet)\. This means that textile producers&#x2F;importers will be legally responsible for the costs of textile collection, reuse, and recycling\. According to LSJH, this pending policy **“shifts the responsibility \(and costs\) for textile recycling to producers”**, undermining the business case for a municipally\-run refinement plant[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/eun-tuottajavastuuvalmistelu-vesitti-poistotekstiilin-jalostuslaitoksen-rakentamisen-turkuun/#:~:text=%E2%80%93%20Lopullista%20p%C3%A4%C3%A4t%C3%B6ksentekoa%20laitosinvestoinnista%20on,kertoo%20LSJH%3An%20toimitusjohtaja%20Jukka%20Heikkil%C3%A4)[yle\.fi](https://yle.fi/a/74-20085331#:~:text=toistuvasti%20siirtyneet)\. LSJH had delayed final investment decisions while awaiting clarity on this legislation, which was repeatedly postponed\. By early 2024, with the directive nearing completion, it became evident that a fully producer\-funded system would take over the role LSJH had planned to fill[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/eun-tuottajavastuuvalmistelu-vesitti-poistotekstiilin-jalostuslaitoksen-rakentamisen-turkuun/#:~:text=%E2%80%93%20Lopullista%20p%C3%A4%C3%A4t%C3%B6ksentekoa%20laitosinvestoinnista%20on,kertoo%20LSJH%3An%20toimitusjohtaja%20Jukka%20Heikkil%C3%A4)\.
- **Economic Feasibility:** During the planning period, **estimated construction costs for the Turku plant nearly doubled** from the original €20\.5 million, due to inflation and changing requirements[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/eun-tuottajavastuuvalmistelu-vesitti-poistotekstiilin-jalostuslaitoksen-rakentamisen-turkuun/#:~:text=ker%C3%A4ysm%C3%A4%C3%A4r%C3%A4t%20ovat%20j%C3%A4%C3%A4neet%20kauas%20laitoksen,tarpeista%2C%20Jutila%20jatkaa)[yle\.fi](https://yle.fi/a/74-20085331#:~:text=olisi%20edellytt%C3%A4nyt%20varmuutta%20raaka,mukaan%20j%C3%A4%C3%A4neet%20kauas%20laitoksen%20tarpeista)\. At the same time, the volume of textiles collected in the first year of nationwide collection \(2023\) fell far short of the 20,000\-ton capacity – initial collection amounts were “a long way from the plant’s needs,” highlighting a risk of underutilization[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/eun-tuottajavastuuvalmistelu-vesitti-poistotekstiilin-jalostuslaitoksen-rakentamisen-turkuun/#:~:text=%E2%80%93%20Vuodessa%2020%20000%20tonnia,kauas%20laitoksen%20tarpeista%2C%20Jutila%20jatkaa)[yle\.fi](https://yle.fi/a/74-20085331#:~:text=Vuodessa%2020%20000%20tonnia%20poistotekstiili%C3%A4,mukaan%20j%C3%A4%C3%A4neet%20kauas%20laitoksen%20tarpeista)\. LSJH also sought co\-investors or operators to share the burden: they negotiated with **around 25 potential partners** in industry to implement the plant together, but no deal materialized[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/eun-tuottajavastuuvalmistelu-vesitti-poistotekstiilin-jalostuslaitoksen-rakentamisen-turkuun/#:~:text=Ker%C3%A4ys)\. These factors \(cost escalations and uncertain feedstock supply\) further challenged the viability of the project\.
- **Project Halt in 2024:** In April 2024, LSJH’s board and owners made the difficult decision **to cancel the construction of the Topinpuisto textile refinement plant**[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/eun-tuottajavastuuvalmistelu-vesitti-poistotekstiilin-jalostuslaitoksen-rakentamisen-turkuun/#:~:text=Lounais,ole%20toiminnallisia%20tai%20taloudellisia%20edellytyksi%C3%A4)[yle\.fi](https://yle.fi/a/74-20085331#:~:text=Turkuun%20pitk%C3%A4%C3%A4n%20suunniteltu%20poistotekstiilin%20jalostuslaitos,on%20kariutunut)\. LSJH officially announced it will *“luopua\.\.\.20,000 ton capacity post\-consumer textile processing plant”* in Turku, citing the lack of favorable operational and financial conditions[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/eun-tuottajavastuuvalmistelu-vesitti-poistotekstiilin-jalostuslaitoksen-rakentamisen-turkuun/#:~:text=Lounais,ole%20toiminnallisia%20tai%20taloudellisia%20edellytyksi%C3%A4)\. In other words, the evolving EPR scheme and market conditions **“took away the foundation”** for LSJH’s full\-scale facility plan[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/eun-tuottajavastuuvalmistelu-vesitti-poistotekstiilin-jalostuslaitoksen-rakentamisen-turkuun/#:~:text=%E2%80%93%20Lopullista%20p%C3%A4%C3%A4t%C3%B6ksentekoa%20laitosinvestoinnista%20on,kertoo%20LSJH%3An%20toimitusjohtaja%20Jukka%20Heikkil%C3%A4)\. The company concluded that proceeding with such a large investment no longer made sense when producers will soon be mandated to handle textile recycling \(potentially through their own systems or other service providers\)\.
- **Current Operations:** Despite shelving the big plant, LSJH continues its textile waste activities on a smaller scale\. As of 2024, LSJH is **maintaining the national collection and sorting service** for post\-consumer textiles – at least until the end of 2024, after which the transition to a producer\-responsibility system will be reassessed[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/eun-tuottajavastuuvalmistelu-vesitti-poistotekstiilin-jalostuslaitoksen-rakentamisen-turkuun/#:~:text=Jukka%20Heikkil%C3%A4n%20mukaan%20LSJH%20neuvotteli,taloudelliset%20ja%20toiminnalliset%20edellytykset%20investoinnilta)\. In late 2023, LSJH wound down the Paimio pilot line \(the lease ended and the equipment was sold to a private operator\), consolidating all sorting work to its Turku facility[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/LSJH-Vuosikatsaus-2023_nettiin.pdf#:~:text=Tuottajavastuu%20toi%20LSJH%3An%20suunnitteleman%2020,valtakunnallisen%20ker%C3%A4yksen%20koordinointiin%20ja%20lajittelutoiminnan)[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/eun-tuottajavastuuvalmistelu-vesitti-poistotekstiilin-jalostuslaitoksen-rakentamisen-turkuun/#:~:text=%E2%80%93%20Paimion%20pilot,LSJH%3An%20kehitysjohtaja%20Teemu%20Jutila%20sanoo)\. Collected textiles from across Finland are still sent to Turku, where LSJH manually sorts and bales material for recycling\. The sorted outputs \(like cotton\-rich bales\) are supplied as raw material to fiber processors and recycling initiatives in the interim\. LSJH remains involved in R&amp;D and networks \(e\.g\. the **Telaketju** and **tExtended** projects\) to support the emerging textile recycling infrastructure in Finland[telaketju\.turkuamk\.fi](https://telaketju.turkuamk.fi/en/the-telaketju-network/#:~:text=the%20Baltic%20region)[textended\.eu](https://textended.eu/lounais-suomen-jatehuolto/#:~:text=LSJH%E2%80%99s%20role%20in%20tExtended%20concentrates,textiles%20to%20create%20new%20markets)\.

In summary, **LSJH’s textile operations from 2021 to 2024** achieved important milestones in Finland’s circular economy for textiles\. They piloted advanced **NIR\-based sorting technology** and proved that a centralized facility could produce usable recycled fiber from post\-consumer clothing\. The planned Turku plant was backed by significant investment and promised to scale those trials to the national level with a 20,000\-ton yearly capacity[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/eun-tuottajavastuuvalmistelu-vesitti-poistotekstiilin-jalostuslaitoksen-rakentamisen-turkuun/#:~:text=Lounais,ole%20toiminnallisia%20tai%20taloudellisia%20edellytyksi%C3%A4)[global\-recycling\.info](https://global-recycling.info/archives/4180#:~:text=a%20larger%20industrial%20scale%2C%20the,life%20textile%20flows)\. However, shifting legislative and economic factors led LSJH to pause the large investment in 2024\. Moving forward, the responsibility for large\-scale textile sorting and recycling is expected to shift to producer\-led systems under the new EU directive[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/eun-tuottajavastuuvalmistelu-vesitti-poistotekstiilin-jalostuslaitoksen-rakentamisen-turkuun/#:~:text=%E2%80%93%20Lopullista%20p%C3%A4%C3%A4t%C3%B6ksentekoa%20laitosinvestoinnista%20on,kertoo%20LSJH%3An%20toimitusjohtaja%20Jukka%20Heikkil%C3%A4)\. Nonetheless, LSJH’s early efforts – including the collection network and technology development – have laid the groundwork for Finland’s textile recycling ecosystem, and in the meantime LSJH continues to collect and sort textiles to keep resources in circulation as the new system takes shape[lsjh\.fi](https://lsjh.fi/eun-tuottajavastuuvalmistelu-vesitti-poistotekstiilin-jalostuslaitoksen-rakentamisen-turkuun/#:~:text=j%C3%A4tepuitedirektiivi%2C%20joka%20toteutuneessa%20muodossaan%20vei,taloudelliset%20ja%20toiminnalliset%20edellytykset%20investoinnilta)[textended\.eu](https://textended.eu/lounais-suomen-jatehuolto/#:~:text=LSJH%E2%80%99s%20role%20in%20tExtended%20concentrates,textiles%20to%20create%20new%20markets)\.
