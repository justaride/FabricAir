# Finnish and EU Textile Waste Regulatory Timelines

## Finland: Early Separate Collection and Upcoming EPR

**Mandatory Textile Collection \(2023\):** Finland was one of the first EU countries to mandate separate collection of textile waste\. In 2021 the government adopted Waste Act amendments \(Government Decree 978&#x2F;2021\) requiring all municipalities to establish textile waste collection by **1 January 2023**[heyfashion\.org](https://www.heyfashion.org/legislation-tracker/a-new-circular-economy-action-plan-ceap-kjnas-bs4mt-y4y6b-53b6c-cmgsw-nndm4-45byj-7tm7m#:~:text=Ref%20no%3A%20978%2F2021%20Status%3A%20ADOPTED)[resource\.co](https://resource.co/article/helsinki-trial-ahead-eu-mandatory-textile-recycling#:~:text=EU%20law%20stipulates%20that%20the,textiles%20from%20January%20this%20year)\. This is two years ahead of the EU\-wide deadline in 2025 for separate textile collection\. Under the new Finnish rules, from 2023 every municipality must provide an adequate number of drop\-off points for post\-consumer textiles, ensuring these are collected separately for reuse or recycling instead of ending up in mixed waste[resource\.co](https://resource.co/article/helsinki-trial-ahead-eu-mandatory-textile-recycling#:~:text=service)\. The law also extends to businesses: companies that generate significant textile waste are obliged to sort and send it for proper processing rather than disposal[resource\.co](https://resource.co/article/helsinki-trial-ahead-eu-mandatory-textile-recycling#:~:text=service)\. By moving early, Finland aimed to reduce the ~40,000 tonnes of used textiles that were previously ending up in mixed waste \(2019 data\) and being mostly incinerated \(over 60% of used textiles\)[resource\.co](https://resource.co/article/helsinki-trial-ahead-eu-mandatory-textile-recycling#:~:text=Image%3A%20Textile%20waste%20pile%20garmentsAbout,local%20authorities%20in%20the%20UK)\.

 

**Extended Producer Responsibility \(EPR\) from 2027:** Finland’s next major step is implementing extended producer responsibility for textiles in line with new EU legislation\. In September 2025, the EU approved an amendment to the Waste Framework Directive that introduces **mandatory EPR for textiles EU\-wide**, meaning producers must finance and manage textile waste collection and treatment[europarl\.europa\.eu](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/news/en/press-room/20250905IPR30172/parliament-adopts-new-eu-rules-to-reduce-textile-and-food-waste#:~:text=Producers%20that%20make%20textiles%20available,comply%20with%20the%20EPR%20requirements)\. Finland \(like other Member States\) must transpose this directive into national law by **June 2027**, and have the textile EPR system up and running by April 2028 at the latest[ely\-keskus\.fi](https://www.ely-keskus.fi/en/web/tuottajavastuu/etusivu/-/asset_publisher/d7U2T0M5OOPX/content/nyt-se-on-virallista-tekstiilien-tuottajavastuu-alkaa-suomessa-kes%C3%A4ll%C3%A4-2027?p_r_p_assetEntryId=2583654&_com_liferay_asset_publisher_web_portlet_AssetPublisherPortlet_INSTANCE_d7U2T0M5OOPX_type=content&_com_liferay_asset_publisher_web_portlet_AssetPublisherPortlet_INSTANCE_d7U2T0M5OOPX_urlTitle=nyt-se-on-virallista-tekstiilien-tuottajavastuu-alkaa-suomessa-kes%25C3%25A4ll%25C3%25A4-2027&_com_liferay_asset_publisher_web_portlet_AssetPublisherPortlet_INSTANCE_d7U2T0M5OOPX_redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.ely-keskus.fi%2Fen%2Fweb%2Ftuottajavastuu%2Fetusivu%3Fp_p_id%3Dcom_liferay_asset_publisher_web_portlet_AssetPublisherPortlet_INSTANCE_d7U2T0M5OOPX%26p_p_lifecycle%3D0%26p_p_state%3Dnormal%26p_p_mode%3Dview%26p_r_p_assetEntryId%3D2583654%26_com_liferay_asset_publisher_web_portlet_AssetPublisherPortlet_INSTANCE_d7U2T0M5OOPX_cur%3D0%26p_r_p_resetCur%3Dfalse#:~:text=tekstiilien%20j%C3%A4tehuollosta)\. ## EU Requirements: Waste Framework Directive and EPR Mandate

**Separate Collection by 2025:** At the European Union level, the **Waste Framework Directive** \(WFD, as revised in 2018\) requires all Member States to set up separate collection for textiles by **1 January 2025**[eea\.europa\.eu](https://www.eea.europa.eu/en/analysis/publications/management-of-used-and-waste-textiles-in-europes-circular-economy#:~:text=The%20Waste%20Framework%20Directive%20,increasing%20exports%2C%20incineration%2C%20or%20landfilling)\. This EU mandate was part of a broader circular economy push, recognizing that textiles are a significant and growing waste stream\. By 2025, every EU country must have a system for consumers to dispose of used clothing and other textiles separately from general waste, to facilitate reuse and recycling\. Many countries have been preparing for this: as of 2024, more than half of EU states already had some form of mandatory textile collection \(often initially focused on reusables via charity bins\)[eea\.europa\.eu](https://www.eea.europa.eu/en/analysis/publications/management-of-used-and-waste-textiles-in-europes-circular-economy#:~:text=waste%20generated%20from%20manufacturing%20or,unsold%20textiles)\. The EU’s goal is to drastically reduce textile landfill and incineration – currently only about 22% of post\-consumer textile waste in Europe is collected separately for recycling[knowledge\-hub\.circle\-economy\.com](https://knowledge-hub.circle-economy.com/article/8909#:~:text=The%20EU%20imposes%20separate%20textiles,EU%29%202018%2F851)\. Harmonizing definitions and reporting is also in progress to track textile waste flows and set future recycling targets[eea\.europa\.eu](https://www.eea.europa.eu/en/analysis/publications/management-of-used-and-waste-textiles-in-europes-circular-economy#:~:text=that%20significant%20amounts%20of%20collected,to%20regions%20outside%20the%20EU)\.

 

**New EPR Directive \(2025\):** In addition to collection, the EU has moved to make **Extended Producer Responsibility** a cornerstone of textile waste management\. On **9 September 2025**, the European Parliament gave final approval to new rules amending the WFD to include textiles and footwear in EPR schemes[sgs\.com](https://www.sgs.com/en-us/news/2025/09/safeguards-14225-eu-parliament-adopts-new-textile-waste-regulations#:~:text=On%20September%209%2C%202025%2C%20the,and%20kitchen%20linen%20and%20curtains)\. This legislation \(Directive \(EU\) 2025&#x2F;1892\) will obligate all Member States to **“ensure that producers cover the costs of collecting, sorting and recycling textile waste”** by establishing national EPR programs[sgs\.com](https://www.sgs.com/en-us/news/2025/09/safeguards-14225-eu-parliament-adopts-new-textile-waste-regulations#:~:text=Under%20the%20new%20directive%2C%20all,new%20rules%20into%20national%20legislation)\. Key provisions and timelines from the EU directive:

- Member States have **20 months** from the directive’s entry into force to transpose it into national law[sgs\.com](https://www.sgs.com/en-us/news/2025/09/safeguards-14225-eu-parliament-adopts-new-textile-waste-regulations#:~:text=Under%20the%20new%20directive%2C%20all,months%20from%20the%20Directive%E2%80%99s%20entry)[europarl\.europa\.eu](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/news/en/press-room/20250905IPR30172/parliament-adopts-new-eu-rules-to-reduce-textile-and-food-waste#:~:text=the%20EU%20Official%20Journal,the%20rules%20into%20national%20legislation)\. \(The law entered into force on 16 October 2025[ely\-keskus\.fi](https://www.ely-keskus.fi/en/web/tuottajavastuu/etusivu/-/asset_publisher/d7U2T0M5OOPX/content/nyt-se-on-virallista-tekstiilien-tuottajavastuu-alkaa-suomessa-kes%C3%A4ll%C3%A4-2027?p_r_p_assetEntryId=2583654&_com_liferay_asset_publisher_web_portlet_AssetPublisherPortlet_INSTANCE_d7U2T0M5OOPX_type=content&_com_liferay_asset_publisher_web_portlet_AssetPublisherPortlet_INSTANCE_d7U2T0M5OOPX_urlTitle=nyt-se-on-virallista-tekstiilien-tuottajavastuu-alkaa-suomessa-kes%25C3%25A4ll%25C3%25A4-2027&_com_liferay_asset_publisher_web_portlet_AssetPublisherPortlet_INSTANCE_d7U2T0M5OOPX_redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.ely-keskus.fi%2Fen%2Fweb%2Ftuottajavastuu%2Fetusivu%3Fp_p_id%3Dcom_liferay_asset_publisher_web_portlet_AssetPublisherPortlet_INSTANCE_d7U2T0M5OOPX%26p_p_lifecycle%3D0%26p_p_state%3Dnormal%26p_p_mode%3Dview%26p_r_p_assetEntryId%3D2583654%26_com_liferay_asset_publisher_web_portlet_AssetPublisherPortlet_INSTANCE_d7U2T0M5OOPX_cur%3D0%26p_r_p_resetCur%3Dfalse#:~:text=Image%3A%20Piirroskuvassa%20on%20tennarit%2C%20housut,ja%20pyyhe), so transposition will be due by mid\-2027\.\)
- Producers \(including manufacturers, importers, and online sellers placing textiles on the EU market\) will be required to finance the collection, transport, sorting, and treatment of textile waste[europarl\.europa\.eu](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/news/en/press-room/20250905IPR30172/parliament-adopts-new-eu-rules-to-reduce-textile-and-food-waste#:~:text=Producers%20that%20make%20textiles%20available,comply%20with%20the%20EPR%20requirements)\. This includes products like clothing, accessories, footwear, linens, and curtains, with possible extension to mattresses[europarl\.europa\.eu](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/news/en/press-room/20250905IPR30172/parliament-adopts-new-eu-rules-to-reduce-textile-and-food-waste#:~:text=an%20extra%20year%20to%20comply,with%20the%20EPR%20requirements)\. E\-commerce companies and non\-EU producers selling into the EU are also covered to prevent free\-riders[europarl\.europa\.eu](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/news/en/press-room/20250905IPR30172/parliament-adopts-new-eu-rules-to-reduce-textile-and-food-waste#:~:text=Producers%20that%20make%20textiles%20available,comply%20with%20the%20EPR%20requirements)\.
- Each country’s EPR scheme must be **operational within 30 months** of the directive’s entry into force[sgs\.com](https://www.sgs.com/en-us/news/2025/09/safeguards-14225-eu-parliament-adopts-new-textile-waste-regulations#:~:text=Under%20the%20new%20directive%2C%20all,new%20rules%20into%20national%20legislation)\. In practice, that sets a deadline around **early 2028** for all textile producers to be part of a functioning EPR system EU\-wide\. \(Micro\-enterprises get an extra 12\-month grace period\)[sgs\.com](https://www.sgs.com/en-us/news/2025/09/safeguards-14225-eu-parliament-adopts-new-textile-waste-regulations#:~:text=associated%20with%20the%20collection%2C%20sorting,new%20rules%20into%20national%20legislation)\.
- The EPR schemes will feature eco\-modulated fees – meaning producers may pay lower or higher fees depending on the sustainability and recyclability of their products \(to discourage “fast fashion” impacts\)[sgs\.com](https://www.sgs.com/en-us/news/2025/09/safeguards-14225-eu-parliament-adopts-new-textile-waste-regulations#:~:text=The%20Directive%20also%20encourages%20member,contributions%20under%20the%20EPR%20schemes)[europarl\.europa\.eu](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/news/en/press-room/20250905IPR30172/parliament-adopts-new-eu-rules-to-reduce-textile-and-food-waste#:~:text=initiative%2C%20EU%20countries%20may%20also,EPR%20schemes%20for%20mattress%20producers)\. Funds collected will support recycling infrastructure and possibly preparation for reuse, and member states are encouraged to consider measures against ultra\-fast fashion in their fee structures[europarl\.europa\.eu](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/news/en/press-room/20250905IPR30172/parliament-adopts-new-eu-rules-to-reduce-textile-and-food-waste#:~:text=initiative%2C%20EU%20countries%20may%20also,EPR%20schemes%20for%20mattress%20producers)\.

Once implemented, these EU rules mark a significant shift of responsibility onto producers, akin to existing EPR programs for electronics, batteries, or packaging\. This ensures a **unified timeline**: by **2025** separate collection is in place across Europe, and by **2027–2028** producers are taking charge of textile end\-of\-life management\. The overall objective is to incentivize more sustainable product design and to dramatically increase textile reuse and fiber recycling rates, which currently remain below 1% closed\-loop recycling globally[sgs\.com](https://www.sgs.com/en-us/news/2025/09/safeguards-14225-eu-parliament-adopts-new-textile-waste-regulations#:~:text=With%20approximately%205,design%20principles)\.

## Industry and Member State Initiatives

Even before the EU’s blanket EPR requirement, several European countries and industry stakeholders have moved forward with their own textile recycling schemes, creating a patchwork of timelines and regulations:

- **France – Early Pioneer:** France has the longest\-running textile EPR scheme in Europe, established in **2008**\. Under this system, French clothing and textile producers finance a not\-for\-profit organization \(Refashion\) to handle collection and sorting of used textiles\. This “important legacy of EPR” in France has evolved over time to put more emphasis on reuse, repair, and fiber\-to\-fiber recycling, not just collection[circulareconomy\.europa\.eu](https://circulareconomy.europa.eu/platform/sites/default/files/2024-08/Pushing%20the%20boundaries%20of%20EPR%20policy%20for%20textiles.pdf#:~:text=U,to%20evolve%20and%20should%20address)\. France’s early start provided a model, showing that EPR can fund nationwide collection bins and sorting centers; by 2019 France was collecting ~38% of textile waste separately \(though much is exported for reuse\)\. The French scheme has continually raised its ambitions \(e\.g\. financing repair services and R&amp;D\) to align with circular economy goals[circulareconomy\.europa\.eu](https://circulareconomy.europa.eu/platform/sites/default/files/2024-08/Pushing%20the%20boundaries%20of%20EPR%20policy%20for%20textiles.pdf#:~:text=as%20our%20textiles%20EPR%20scheme,significant%20opportunity%20to%20enhance%20transparency)\.
- **Nordic and Western Europe:** **The Netherlands** introduced EPR for textiles effective **1 July 2023**, as part of its circular economy strategy[sustainablebrandplatform\.com](https://www.sustainablebrandplatform.com/articles/eu-country-specific-fashion-texile-regulations-2024#:~:text=The%20Decree%20on%20Extended%20Producer,waste%20in%20the%20Dutch%20market)\. Dutch producers and importers of apparel and home textiles must now fund collection and meet reuse&#x2F;recycling targets – for example, at least 50% of textiles collected should be reused or recycled by 2025 \(with specific sub\-targets for reuse and fiber\-to\-fiber recycling\)[sustainablebrandplatform\.com](https://www.sustainablebrandplatform.com/articles/eu-country-specific-fashion-texile-regulations-2024#:~:text=The%20scheme%20covers%20newly%20manufactured,fiber%20recycling)\. The Dutch system requires producers to set up take\-back infrastructure and supports innovative recycling projects to meet these targets[sustainablebrandplatform\.com](https://www.sustainablebrandplatform.com/articles/eu-country-specific-fashion-texile-regulations-2024#:~:text=wear%2C%20and%20household%20linen,fiber%20recycling)\. **Sweden** announced plans for a textile EPR as early as 2020 and has been developing its scheme \(originally aiming for a 2024 start\), focusing on building sorting capacity and integrating with the existing second\-hand collection network[sustainablebrandplatform\.com](https://sustainablebrandplatform.com/articles/eu-country-specific-fashion-texile-regulations-2024#:~:text=EU%20Country,environmental%20outcomes%20and%20hold)\. **Spain** passed a waste law in 2022 that mandates separate textile collection by 2025 and calls for a **textile EPR scheme to be established within 3 years** \(by roughly 2025\)[sustainablebrandplatform\.com](https://www.sustainablebrandplatform.com/articles/eu-country-specific-fashion-texile-regulations-2024#:~:text=,for%20textiles%20within%203%20years)\. **Greece** similarly legislated a ban on textile landfilling from 2024 and required a Producer Responsibility Organization for textiles by end of 2023[sustainablebrandplatform\.com](https://www.sustainablebrandplatform.com/articles/eu-country-specific-fashion-texile-regulations-2024#:~:text=In%202021%2C%20Greece%20introduced%20the,includes%20two%20key%20textile%20provisions), although details of its EPR system are still being ironed out\. These national efforts indicate that, by the mid\-2020s, multiple EU countries were already on track to have textile EPR either in force or imminent, even before the EU law made it obligatory\.
- **Central&#x2F;Eastern Europe:** Some smaller EU members have moved quickly too\. **Latvia** and **Hungary** are noted among the early adopters with **national EPR frameworks for textiles already implemented by 2023\-2024**[pincvision\.com](https://www.pincvision.com/news/textile-epr-what-is-to-be-expected#:~:text=In%20the%20draft%20amendment%20of,when%20the%20amendment%20becomes%20final)\. For instance, Hungary included textiles in its producer responsibility regime in 2023, and Latvia launched a textile collection and recycling program via its existing waste management system\. These early movers are likely to form the first wave of compliance as the EU directive comes into effect\. Meanwhile, others without existing schemes will need to catch up by 2027\. Industry groups, like the European textile association EURATEX, have been coordinating forums to **harmonize EPR approaches** so that differences between national systems \(e\.g\. what products are covered, fee structures\) do not impede the EU single market[transition\-pathways\.europa\.eu](https://transition-pathways.europa.eu/textiles/news/eu-industry-forum-reaches-milestone-harmonising-textile-epr#:~:text=pathways,EPR%29%20across%20Europe)\. Overall, as of late 2025, at least **four EU countries \(France, Netherlands, Latvia, Hungary\)** already have binding textile EPR requirements in place, and several others are in advanced stages of planning, ensuring a readiness to meet the EU’s timeline[pincvision\.com](https://www.pincvision.com/news/textile-epr-what-is-to-be-expected#:~:text=In%20the%20draft%20amendment%20of,when%20the%20amendment%20becomes%20final)\.

## Implementation Status in Finland \(2023–2025\)

Finland’s proactive policies have translated into on\-the\-ground changes in how textile waste is handled:

- **National Collection Network:** Since January 2023, a **nationwide network of 25 municipal waste management companies** has been responsible for collecting household end\-of\-life textiles, together covering about 95% of Finland’s population[naturvardsverket\.se](https://www.naturvardsverket.se/4ade32/contentassets/f649d5409dac431ba8c215b0544848b5/separate-end-of-life-textile-collection-sorting-and-solutions-learnings-from-finland.pdf#:~:text=%E2%80%A2%20Separate%20collection%20requirement%20for,Nationwide%20municipal%20collection)\. This means residents across virtually all regions now have access to textile recycling points \(for example, dedicated containers at recycling centers or drop\-off bins in public locations\)\. Separately collected textiles must be sent for processing with priority to reuse and high\-quality recycling \(such as fiber\-to\-fiber\)[resource\.co](https://resource.co/article/helsinki-trial-ahead-eu-mandatory-textile-recycling#:~:text=service)\. The early results show strong participation: municipalities quickly ramped up collection infrastructure, in part building on pilot projects and existing charity collection schemes\.
- **Pilot Programs and Collection Volumes:** Even before the mandate kicked in, Finnish regions began trial collections\. In the capital region \(Helsinki area\), the waste authority HSY ran pilot programs in 2019–2022 to test collection methods\. By 2021, the average person in Helsinki produced **3\.11 kg of textile waste per year** that was captured in these separate collection pilots[resource\.co](https://resource.co/article/helsinki-trial-ahead-eu-mandatory-textile-recycling#:~:text=While%20the%20legal%20requirement%20is,%E2%80%93%20subject%20to%20a%20charge)\. The pilots tried different models – e\.g\. a paid drop\-off at waste stations vs\. free collection bins in shopping centers – and found the free, easily accessible bins yielded much better results \(with significantly higher reuse&#x2F;recycling rates and less contamination\)[resource\.co](https://resource.co/article/helsinki-trial-ahead-eu-mandatory-textile-recycling#:~:text=dirty%20or%20wet%20during%20transportation)\. These learnings guided the rollout of the 2023 national system\. As of the latest data, Helsinki has expanded to eleven official textile collection points and is planning more, balancing convenience with costs[resource\.co](https://resource.co/article/helsinki-trial-ahead-eu-mandatory-textile-recycling#:~:text=cent%20of%20Finnish%20end,per%20year)\. Nationwide figures are climbing: thousands of tonnes of textiles have been diverted from trash in the first year of the program, though exact national tonnage for 2023 is still being compiled\. The **trend** is clear – Finland’s head start means it is already collecting a substantial share of textile waste that would otherwise have been incinerated or landfilled, moving closer to a circular model\.
- **Recycling and Processing Capacity:** A challenge that comes with increased collection is handling the material\. Finland has invested in domestic sorting and recycling capabilities, notably through **Lounais\-Suomen Jätehuolto \(LSJH\)**, the southwest Finland waste company leading textile recycling\. LSJH operates a **central sorting facility in Turku** which receives textile bales from around the country[resource\.co](https://resource.co/article/helsinki-trial-ahead-eu-mandatory-textile-recycling#:~:text=The%20collected%20end,for%20further%20sorting%20and%20processing)\. There, textiles are quality\-checked, sorted by material \(using manual and automated methods\), and processed into recycled fiber\. The LSJH **mechanical recycling plant** can currently process about *10% of all Finland’s end\-of\-life textiles per year* into fiber[resource\.co](https://resource.co/article/helsinki-trial-ahead-eu-mandatory-textile-recycling#:~:text=The%20collected%20end,for%20further%20sorting%20and%20processing)\. While this is a significant start, it also highlights the need to scale up capacity – as collections grow \(and will grow further once producer responsibility kicks in\), Finland will either need to expand local recycling infrastructure or export some of the sorted textiles to recycling facilities elsewhere in Europe\. Plans are underway to increase capacity, including exploring chemical recycling for textiles that are not suitable for mechanical fiber recovery[naturvardsverket\.se](https://www.naturvardsverket.se/4ade32/contentassets/f649d5409dac431ba8c215b0544848b5/separate-end-of-life-textile-collection-sorting-and-solutions-learnings-from-finland.pdf#:~:text=%E2%80%A2%20Nationwide%20network%20of%2025,mechanical%2C%20chemical%20and%20thermal%20processes)[resource\.co](https://resource.co/article/helsinki-trial-ahead-eu-mandatory-textile-recycling#:~:text=LSJH%20mechanically%20processes%20end,life%20textiles%20per%20year)\.
- **Interim Outcomes:** The early implementation in Finland has been praised as a **circular economy success story**, but it also exposes practical issues to address before 2027\. On the positive side, consumer awareness of textile recycling has risen, and municipalities report strong engagement with the new collection system\. Finland is **ahead of schedule** relative to EU requirements, providing a testing ground for the EPR phase\. On the other hand, financing the collection and processing is currently falling on municipal waste budgets and taxpayers; the forthcoming EPR will shift these costs to producers\. Waste companies like LSJH have had to adjust their business plans, knowing that by 2027 a producer\-run system will take over national coordination[epressi\.com](https://www.epressi.com/tiedotteet/ymparisto-ja-luonto/poistotekstiileille-eussa-saadetty-tuottajavastuu-muuttaa-lsjhn-roolia-tekstiilien-kiertotaloudessa.html#:~:text=Lounais,toimialueensa%20poistotekstiilin%20ker%C3%A4yksest%C3%A4%20ja%20k%C3%A4sittelyst%C3%A4)[epressi\.com](https://www.epressi.com/tiedotteet/ymparisto-ja-luonto/poistotekstiileille-eussa-saadetty-tuottajavastuu-muuttaa-lsjhn-roolia-tekstiilien-kiertotaloudessa.html#:~:text=Tuottajavastuu%20muutosten%20taustalla)\. Indeed, LSJH announced in 2025 that it will scale back its own textile circular economy operations \(like a planned large sorting plant\) because the **producer responsibility regime will reshape roles and funding** in a few years[epressi\.com](https://www.epressi.com/tiedotteet/ymparisto-ja-luonto/poistotekstiileille-eussa-saadetty-tuottajavastuu-muuttaa-lsjhn-roolia-tekstiilien-kiertotaloudessa.html#:~:text=kehitt%C3%A4miseen%20liittyvist%C3%A4%20liiketoiminnoistaan,toimialueensa%20poistotekstiilin%20ker%C3%A4yksest%C3%A4%20ja%20k%C3%A4sittelyst%C3%A4)[epressi\.com](https://www.epressi.com/tiedotteet/ymparisto-ja-luonto/poistotekstiileille-eussa-saadetty-tuottajavastuu-muuttaa-lsjhn-roolia-tekstiilien-kiertotaloudessa.html#:~:text=Tuottajavastuu%20muutosten%20taustalla)\. This illustrates the transition period challenge: building collection systems now that seamlessly integrate with producer\-driven schemes later\. Finnish authorities are actively coordinating with industry and neighboring countries \(through Nordic and Baltic cooperation forums\) to ensure that the 2027 EPR rollout will build on the successful collection foundation already in place, without disrupting services to the public[facebook\.com](https://www.facebook.com/pirkanmaanely/videos/tuottajavastuun-uutiskirje-42025-on-ilmestynyt-aiheina-mm-%EF%B8%8F-lupa-ja-valvontavira/886653720352622/#:~:text=Tuottajavastuun%20uutiskirje%204%2F2025%20on%20ilmestynyt%21,%E2%99%BB%EF%B8%8F%20Tekstiilien%20tuottajavastuu%20alkaa)[epressi\.com](https://www.epressi.com/tiedotteet/ymparisto-ja-luonto/poistotekstiileille-eussa-saadetty-tuottajavastuu-muuttaa-lsjhn-roolia-tekstiilien-kiertotaloudessa.html#:~:text=Toiminnan%20supistamisen%20taustalla%20vaikuttavat%20lains%C3%A4%C3%A4d%C3%A4nn%C3%B6lliset,entisest%C3%A4%C3%A4n%20haastavan%20liiketoiminnan%20kannattavuuskysymyksen%20ratkaisemista)\.

In summary, **Finland’s timeline** shows an ambitious acceleration of textile waste policy – mandatory collection in 2023 and full producer responsibility by 2027 – which dovetails with the **broader EU timeline** \(2025 separate collection across all member states, and EPR schemes by 2027–2028 Europe\-wide\)\. Legislative references like Finland’s Waste Act decree and the EU’s new directive \(EU 2025&#x2F;1892\) anchor these dates in law[heyfashion\.org](https://www.heyfashion.org/legislation-tracker/a-new-circular-economy-action-plan-ceap-kjnas-bs4mt-y4y6b-53b6c-cmgsw-nndm4-45byj-7tm7m#:~:text=Ref%20no%3A%20978%2F2021%20Status%3A%20ADOPTED)[ely\-keskus\.fi](https://www.ely-keskus.fi/en/web/tuottajavastuu/etusivu/-/asset_publisher/d7U2T0M5OOPX/content/nyt-se-on-virallista-tekstiilien-tuottajavastuu-alkaa-suomessa-kes%C3%A4ll%C3%A4-2027?p_r_p_assetEntryId=2583654&_com_liferay_asset_publisher_web_portlet_AssetPublisherPortlet_INSTANCE_d7U2T0M5OOPX_type=content&_com_liferay_asset_publisher_web_portlet_AssetPublisherPortlet_INSTANCE_d7U2T0M5OOPX_urlTitle=nyt-se-on-virallista-tekstiilien-tuottajavastuu-alkaa-suomessa-kes%25C3%25A4ll%25C3%25A4-2027&_com_liferay_asset_publisher_web_portlet_AssetPublisherPortlet_INSTANCE_d7U2T0M5OOPX_redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.ely-keskus.fi%2Fen%2Fweb%2Ftuottajavastuu%2Fetusivu%3Fp_p_id%3Dcom_liferay_asset_publisher_web_portlet_AssetPublisherPortlet_INSTANCE_d7U2T0M5OOPX%26p_p_lifecycle%3D0%26p_p_state%3Dnormal%26p_p_mode%3Dview%26p_r_p_assetEntryId%3D2583654%26_com_liferay_asset_publisher_web_portlet_AssetPublisherPortlet_INSTANCE_d7U2T0M5OOPX_cur%3D0%26p_r_p_resetCur%3Dfalse#:~:text=tekstiilien%20j%C3%A4tehuollosta)\. The implementation on the ground is ongoing: collection networks are expanding, recycling technologies are being tested, and producers are preparing to take on their upcoming responsibilities\. Both in Finland and the EU, these measures aim to significantly reduce textile waste and spur a more sustainable textile industry by the end of the decade\.
